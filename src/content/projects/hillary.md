Hillary Cosmetics sells natural and organic cosmetics to the Polish market and beyond — a category where the product story matters as much as the purchase flow. The brand needed a store that could carry both.

The build scope was broad by design. Warehouse connections, local Polish payment systems, a Romanian market extension, bundle product logic, multi-language configuration, email flows, and a range of custom metafield and collection structures — all of it needed to work together from day one.

Building for the Polish market adds friction that standard Shopify setups don't anticipate. Payment infrastructure, regulatory context, and shopper expectations don't map cleanly onto global defaults. The integrations required deliberate selection and configuration, not out-of-the-box installation.

The project ran in clearly defined phases. Each stage was scoped in writing, reviewed in a call, and signed off before the next began. That structure kept the build on track and gave the client full visibility at every point — no surprises, no scope drift.

Hillary launched with a store that was complete: two markets active, three languages configured, warehouses live, and email flows running. Built for where they operate, not just where Shopify assumes they do.
