In 2024 everyone talked about AI. In 2025 everyone experimented with AI. In 2026, the agencies that figured it out are operating differently — and it shows in their margins.

Here's what we've actually learned integrating AI agents into client workflows.

## What "AI Agent" Actually Means in Practice

An AI agent is software that can take a goal, break it into steps, use tools, and complete the task with minimal human intervention.

In a web agency context, that looks like:

- A client submits a new blog post brief → agent researches, drafts, formats, and pushes to Webflow CMS
- A new lead fills in a contact form → agent qualifies it, enriches with company data, creates a CRM entry, and sends a personalised first response
- A site goes live → agent runs Lighthouse, checks for broken links, generates a performance report, and emails the client

None of these require a developer. They require **setup** — which is where agencies come in.

## The Three Categories of AI Work

After experimenting with dozens of workflows, we bucket AI tasks into three categories:

### 1. Fully Automatable
Clear inputs, clear outputs, no judgment required. Just run it.

- Content formatting and publishing
- Image alt-text generation
- Metadata generation from content
- Weekly analytics summaries

### 2. Human-in-the-Loop
AI drafts or processes, human reviews and approves.

- First draft of client reports
- Proposal generation from a brief
- Code review suggestions

### 3. AI-Assisted (Not Automatable)
AI accelerates the work but can't replace the judgment.

- Client strategy calls
- Design decisions
- Technical architecture choices

:::tip
Start with Category 1. Pick one fully automatable workflow, build it, measure the time saved. Once you trust the system, expand.
:::

## The Real Opportunity: Client Busywork

The most underrated AI opportunity for web agencies isn't building AI features — it's eliminating the friction in client relationships.

Think about what your team spends time on that isn't building:

- Writing status update emails
- Formatting feedback from clients into actionable tasks
- Generating monthly reports
- Answering the same questions in discovery calls

We automated our status update emails six months ago. It saves one person about 3 hours per week. Small — but it's 3 hours now spent on actual building.

:::info
The best automation isn't the most technically impressive. It's the one you actually run every day.
:::

## What Clients Are Actually Asking For

In 2026 we're seeing clients request three types of AI work:

1. **AI-powered chatbots on their site** — usually built with a third-party tool (Intercom, Crisp) connected to their knowledge base
2. **Automated content workflows** — brief → AI draft → human review → publish to CMS
3. **Lead enrichment and routing** — form submission triggers AI qualification and CRM workflow

The third is where the real ROI is, and where most agencies aren't equipped to help yet.

## How to Position Your Agency

You don't need to become an AI company. You need to be an agency that doesn't ignore AI.

The positioning that works: *"We build websites that connect to your workflows — including AI tools."*

That means:
- Being fluent in Make.com / Zapier + AI modules
- Knowing when to recommend a third-party AI tool vs building a custom integration
- Being able to demo a simple AI workflow to a client in a discovery call
